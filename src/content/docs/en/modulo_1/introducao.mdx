## Introduction & Concepts

During the scientific research process, we need to handle an ever-increasing amount of information. This data must be properly cataloged for future reference.

In addition, style guides, such as ABNT *(Brazilian Technical Standards Association
)*, require a series of rules for correct citation and formatting of the reference page. When we add teamwork to this equation, keeping everything organized manually becomes quite a complex task.

:::note[Reference Management Software]
To solve this, we use **Citation Management Software (CMS)** or Reference Management Software. They are designed to automate citation formatting and, most importantly: **centralize your research references in a single place**.
:::

This *software* allows you to save references from **any** type of source: scientific articles, books, web pages, videos, and even podcasts. Additionally, it allows you to add notes, summaries, and files together, making your research material more organized and accessible.

### What are Metadata?

CMS use **metadata** to organize references. Simply put, metadata is "data about data"that is, information describing the contents of a file.  
Its central purpose is to make data *discoverable* and *accessible* for future use.  

For example:
* A scientific article PDF contains metadata such as title, authors, publication year, DOI, and abstract.
* A physical book has metadata such as title, author, publisher, publication year, and ISBN.
* A web page can have metadata such as title, URL, access date, and content description.

- [ ] B) The DOI (Digital Object Identifier).

**Answer: C) The full text of the article's introduction.**

*Why?* Remember: metadata is the "label" that describes the file. The introduction text is the file's content itself, not the description!

There are several reference management tools available; however, Zotero stands out for being **free, open-source, and cross-platform**.  

> Zotero is open-source software, which means its development is carried out by a global community of users and developers. Currently, it is a project of the [Digital Scholarship Foundation](https://digitalscholar.org/), a non-profit organization.
