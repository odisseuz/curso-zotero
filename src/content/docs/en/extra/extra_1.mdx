<details>
<summary>
  <strong>Interaction with PDFs and Web Pages</strong> <Badge text="Important" variant="danger"/>
</summary>

Zotero features a built-in reader that allows active annotations without needing external programs.

**Main Features:**
* **Colored Highlights:** Highlight text using different colors (e.g., yellow for key concepts, red for methodology). The color scheme is preserved in the annotations section, allowing you to filter and locate specific categories of information later. (in the sidebar accessible on the left side of the PDF reader)
* **Annotations (Sticky Notes):** Add comments and reflections to specific paragraphs within the text.
* **Image Capture:** The area selection tool allows you to crop charts, tables, or figures from the PDF. The image is saved directly into your note.
* **Web Page Snapshots:** When you save a website that does not have a PDF, Zotero creates a local copy of the page. This guarantees offline access to the original text, even if the website changes, and lets you highlight it exactly as you would with a PDF document.

**Extracting Annotations:**
After reading, you can consolidate all annotations into a single file:
1. Return to your library and right-click on the read article.
2. Select the option **Add Note from Annotations**.
3. Zotero will automatically generate a structured document. This note compiles all your highlights, retaining the colors used, comments, captured images, and page numbers for each citation.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Zotero Groups: Collaborative References</strong></summary>

To use collaborative features and cloud synchronization, the first step is to have an account linked to the application.

**1. Account Creation and Setup**
* Create a free account by visiting the official website (zotero.org/user/register).
* In the desktop app, go to **Edit > Preferences** (on Windows/Linux) or **Zotero > Settings** (on Mac).
* Access the **Sync** tab and enter your credentials. From this moment on, your references will begin syncing.

**2. Managing Groups**
Zotero allows the creation of shared libraries to organize references and documents as a team.
* **Creation and Access:** Creating new groups, as well as sending and accepting invitations, is done directly through your account dashboard on the Zotero website, under the "Groups" tab.
* **Group Types:** Groups can be Private (accessible by invitation only) or Public (with different levels of read and edit permissions).
* **Application Interface:** After joining a group, a new section named **Group Libraries** will appear in the left sidebar of your desktop app, located right below your personal library.
* **Collaboration:** Members with editing permission can add articles, attach PDFs, and create notes. Every change made within this shared library is automatically updated for the rest of the team members.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Zotero Mobile: Reading on Mobile or Tablet</strong></summary>

Zotero has official mobile applications (available for iOS and Android), allowing access to your library and reading articles away from your desktop environment.

**Main Features:**
* **Library Syncing:** When logging in with your Zotero account, your entire structure of collections, items, and tags is mirrored on your mobile device.
* **Integrated Reading and Annotation:** The mobile app includes a native PDF reader. Any markings, highlights, or comments made on your phone or tablet screen sync automatically with your desktop app.
* **Mobile Browser Capture:** You can save new references directly from your mobile browser. Simply use the operating system's native share feature and select the Zotero app as the destination.

**Storage Management:**
To view and highlight PDFs on mobile devices, the files need to be synchronized in the cloud. A free Zotero account provides 300 MB of storage. If your total volume of PDFs exceeds this limit, attachment file syncing will be paused. In these cases, it is necessary to purchase a storage plan or set up an alternative server, such as WebDAV.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Community Plugins</strong></summary>

Zotero is open source, allowing third-party developers to create extensions (plugins) to add new features to the program.

**How to Install:**
1. Download the plugin file (usually with a `.xpi` extension) from the developer's repository (such as GitHub).
2. In Zotero, go to **Tools > Plugins**.
3. Click the gear icon in the top right corner and select **Install Add-on From File...**.
4. Select the downloaded `.xpi` file and restart Zotero.

**Popular Examples:**
* **ZotFile:** Essential for automatically renaming your PDFs based on author and year, and for extracting annotations made in external readers.
* **Better BibTeX:** Fundamental for those who write using LaTeX or Markdown (such as in Obsidian), improving citation key generation and stability.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong> WebDAV: Alternative Storage</strong></summary>

Zotero's free tier for attachment storage (PDFs) is only 300 MB. An excellent free, official alternative to expand this limit is setting up a **WebDAV** server.

WebDAV is a file transfer protocol natively supported by Zotero. In practice, this means you can connect your library to alternative cloud storage services to save heavy PDFs, saving space on your main account.

There are several storage options on the market supporting this technology. To exemplify setup, below is a step-by-step guide using **Koofr**, which is one of the services natively supporting WebDAV for free and reliably.

<details>
<summary><strong>Step-by-step: Setting up Koofr in Zotero</strong></summary>

**1. Create a Zotero Account**  
If you haven't already, sign up for a free account at [Zotero.org](https://www.zotero.org/).

**2. Create a Koofr Account**  
Next, create your cloud storage account at [Koofr](https://koofr.eu/). 

**3. Generate an App Password in Koofr (Required)**  
For security reasons, Koofr requires a dedicated app password specifically for Zotero:
* Log in to your Koofr account via your web browser.
* Navigate to **Preferences** > **Password**.
* Scroll down to the **App Passwords** section.
* Type `Zotero` into the name field and click **Generate**. 
* **Copy the generated password** (you will need it for the next step).

**4. Connect Koofr to Zotero**  
Now, configure Zotero to sync your files using your new Koofr storage:
* Open Zotero and go to your **Sync** settings.
* Under the *File Syncing* section, change the sync option from **Zotero** to **WebDAV**.
* **Set the URL:** Zotero already provides `https://` in the dropdown box. In the text field next to it, type exactly: `app.koofr.net/dav/Koofr`
* Enter your Koofr email address.
* Paste the **App Password** you generated in Step 3 into the password field *(Note: Do not use your standard Koofr login password)*.
* Click **Verify Server**. 
* When prompted, confirm the creation of the `zotero` folder.

You're all set! 

</details>

:::tip[Physical Backup on a USB Drive]
You can also save everything to a USB flash drive! Since we've already covered how to export, simply export your entire library to your flash drive and you'll have a secure, physical backup of all your references.
:::
