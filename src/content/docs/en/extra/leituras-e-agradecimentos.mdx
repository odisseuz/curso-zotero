## Further reading

To explore the topics covered in this course in greater depth and learn about other features, consult these resources:

- [Official Zotero documentation](https://www.zotero.org/support/): a complete reference for installation, library organization, citations, synchronization, and other features.
- [Quick Start Guide](https://www.zotero.org/support/quick_start_guide): a practical review of the application's essential features.
- [Knowledge Base](https://www.zotero.org/support/kb): answers to frequently asked questions and common problems.
- [Zotero Forums](https://forums.zotero.org/): a place to search for solutions and talk with users, experts, and developers.
- [Zotero Blog](https://www.zotero.org/blog/): news, releases, and updates from the project.
- [Better BibTeX documentation](https://retorque.re/zotero-better-bibtex/): resources for integrating Zotero into writing workflows using LaTeX, Markdown, or academic editors.
- [Citation Style Language](https://citationstyles.org/): information about the open language Zotero uses to format citations and bibliographies.

:::tip[Keep practicing]
Choose a real research project, organize its references into a collection, review the metadata, and generate a short bibliography. Practice is the best way to consolidate the workflow presented in this course.
:::
